(Pte) Ltd or Pte Ltd Singapore Private Limited company ((私人)有限公司) Denotes a company owned privately.
Unltd or Ultd England & Wales Unlimited or Anghyfyngedig (Welsh) Private Unlimited Company (with share capital) Unlimited Company (with share capital); as the word implies, the members or shareholders of the company have no limit placed on their liability for the company's financial obligations in the event of it becoming insolvent, however, it is not a requirement under the Companies Act of Great Britain for an Unlimited Company to state either Unltd, Ultd or Unlimited after its company name. It is nevertheless, a company (a legal entity like its peers Ltd and Plc) registered and formed (incorporated) under the Companies Act of Great Britain and subject to the Companies Act. The advantage of an unlimited company is that they are exempted from filing accounts with the Registrar of Companies for public disclosure (unless, during the accounting period covered, the company is a qualified subsidiary or a parent of a limited company undertaking) subject to a few exceptions to this rule.
SE European Union and European Economic Area Societas Europaea (Latin for 'European Company') European (Public) Limited Company (liability limited by share capital) The legal form of the European (Public) Company. Similar to a UK PLC or German AG company etc. An SE (Societas Europaea) may be created on registration in any one of the Member States of the European Union or European Economic Area, and is subject to the European Company Statute - Council Regulation (EC) No 2157/2001 on the Statute for a European company (the 'Regulation'). An SE is able to relocate its registered office to any other Member State of the European Union or European Economic Area, without it being wound up.
Sdn Bhd Malaysia Sendirian Berhad Private limited Company Equivalent to Private Limited Company in the UK

Ltd. Co.

 U.S.A. Limited liability company, similar to a corporation and a limited liability partnership. May be legally abbreviated in multiple ways. The most common abbreviation is LLC. Allowable abbreviations may vary by U.S. State.

Note: Ltd. by itself is not a valid abbreviation for an LLC, because in some states, like Texas, Ltd. may mean a Corporation instead of an LLC.
LLP U.S.A. Limited liability partnership where each partner is fully liable for the debts of the partnership, not including acts of professional negligence or malpractice.
Ltd. UK and others Private company with liability limited by shares (not traded publicly) or by guarantee. Established under English law and used by countries with similar company laws. In Australia, a company with the suffix Ltd may be a company limited by guarantee (e.g. for charities, universities etc). Such companies may obtain dispensation from the Registrar of Companies to operate without the suffix to distinguish their activities from those of a commercial company.
TNHH/ Cty TNHH Vietnam Công ty trách nhiệm hữu hạn. Limited liability company.
Ltda. Brazil Sociedade Limitada. Limited liability partnership where each partner is fully liable for the debts of the partnership.
NL Australia Mining company not entitled to call on the unpaid issue price of shares; no liability for shareholders, as distinct from limited liability
PLLC United States Professional Limited Liability Company. Some states do not allow certain professionals to form an LLC that would limit the liability that results from the services the professionals provide such as doctors, medical care; lawyers, legal advice; and accountants, accounting services, when the company formed offers the services of the professionals. Instead states allow a PLLC or in the LLC statutes, the liability limitation only applies to the business side, such as creditors of the company, as opposed to the service side, the level of medical care, legal services, or accounting provided to clients. This is meant to maintain the higher ethical standards that these professionals have committed themselves to by becoming licensed in their profession and not immune to malpractice suits.
PLC UK and Ireland A public limited company, liability limited by shares which are traded publicly.
OOD / ООД Bulgaria Limited liability company (drujestvo s ogranichena otgovornost, Дружество с Ограничена Отговорност). Similar to Ltd and GmbH.

Pty. / Pte. Various a prefix used to distinguish a Proprietary Limited company (Pty. Ltd.), essentially the same as a UK private limited company (Ltd.) Used in Australia, South Africa, Singapore and others. A Proprietary company generally (depending on jurisdiction) is one with fewer than 50 members (shareholders), and not exceeding a certain annual revenue. In Australia, proprietary companies are classified as either small or large depending on the number of employees and the quantum of gross assets and operating capital.
